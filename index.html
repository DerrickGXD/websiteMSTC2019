About the event

The Malaysian Students’ Technology Conference (MSTC) is the first initiative spearheaded by Malaysian students studying in the UK that serves as a platform for topics that are relevant to STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) students.

Under the banner of Young Malaysian Engineers (YME) the conference will cover a wide range of topics within the scope of entrepreneurship, social, environment and corporate. Not only will the participants get to have better insights on the local challenges faced in Malaysia, they will also have the opportunity to network with people from the industry.

About YME

Young Malaysian Engineers (YME) is a United Kingdom-based student organization built to empower Malaysian youth in the fields of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Maths (STEM). Founded in 2016, YME is one of the fastest-growing, STEM-oriented student bodies with 200+ registered members and a combined 2000+ followers on all social media platforms.

Mission, Vision and Objectives

Mission

To gather a group of motivated and passionate students through a platform; providing them with insights into current and future technological trends and allowing them to interact with industrial experts to advance their ideas while exploring learning and career opportunities in STEM.

Mission and Vision

Vision

To transform the event into an interactive conference that creates a sustainable, long-term impact on the perspectives of Malaysian youth on STEM developments in Malaysia.

Objectives

To connect students to industrial leaders in the field and equip students the bigger picture of learning and career opportunities in STEM.

To inspire students to apply their knowledge in STEM and provide platform for collaboration to advance their ideas.

.To explore the current and future trends in technology applied in the real world

Dr. Jasmine Hau Yuan Wen

Topic: Medical Technology

Dr. Hau Yuan Wen is a research fellow and the head of Cardiac Informatics Cluster at UTM-IJN Cardiovascular Engineering Centre, also a senior lecturer at the School of Biomedical Engineering and Health Sciences, Faculty of Engineering, Universiti Teknologi Malaysia. She received both Ph.D. degree and master degree in electrical engineering from Universiti Teknologi Malaysia in 2009 and 2005, respectively. Dr. Hau's research interests are in the design of multi-processor systems-on-chip (MPSoC) /networks-on-chip (NoC), from high level algorithm modelling until low level of processing architecture design, as well as field programmable gate array (FPGA) implementation. From there her research interests have spread into the field of cryptographic and data security application, biomedical application such as arrhythmia classification and medical imaging processing, as well as ADHD/autism assessment. She has published more than 50 papers in peer reviewed international journals and conferences, and several book chapters. She has won many best invention awards in design competition, as well as best paper publication award in many international conferences. She is also a member of the IEEE.

Koh Wyhow

Topic: Data Analytics

Koh Wyhow is a data analytics professional with an excess of 5 years of experience, who obtained his Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from NUS. His work involves providing advanced analytics and business intelligence solutions for airlines, media, property and FMCG industries using various data science techniques such as Decision Trees, Random Forest and many more.

• Helped steer Pakatan Harapan to victory in GE14

• Developed forecasting models to model future job vacancies, identify prospective customers, electoral results and many more.

• 99% of students achieving a minimum of A whilst teaching in Taylors College.

Dr. Moonyati Yatid

Topic: Rise of Machines

Dr Moonyati is Senior Analyst in the Technology, Innovation, Environment and Sustainability Programme of the Institute of Strategic and International Studies (ISIS) Malaysia. Her research focuses on issues related to technological advancements and innovation, with interests on digital economy, Artificial Intelligence (A.I), Internet of Things (IoT) and SME development. Her past projects include consultation work for the World Bank Group on the innovation terrain in Malaysia. Previously, she drove efforts on organisational improvements in the oil and gas industry, which comprises change management, resource optimisation and business improvement initiatives. She holds a Ph.D. in Human-Computer Interaction from University of Sydney, Australia where her works focused on human behaviours and their correlations with collaborative software and hardware tools. She obtained her bachelor’s degree of Engineering in Design and Information Sciences from Wakayama University, Japan, with a dissertation on integrating culture in designing groupware.
